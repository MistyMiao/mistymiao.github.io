I am a master's student in the Communication Data Science Dual-degree Program at Tsinghua University and the University of Southern California. I received my B.A. in Communication from Sun Yat-sen University, where I also previously studied psychology.

My research is driven by a curiosity about the **psychological mechanisms** underlying communication phenomena. I am particularly interested in **media psychology**, **evolutionary psychology**, **media effects**, and **misinformation sharing**. Methodologically, I am drawn both to **designing experiments** that isolate causal processes and to **exploring computational methods** for analyzing large-scale communication data.

My current projects examine why people share misinformation, how individuals perceive the effects of violent media content, and how psychological predictors can be used to understand public attitudes toward surveillance and content moderation.

You can find more about my ongoing work on the [Research](/publications/) page, and download my CV from the [CV](/cv/) page.
